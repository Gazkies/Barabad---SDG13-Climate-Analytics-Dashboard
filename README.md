## 🧐 The Investigative Problem
*“What structural factors influence **Climate Action (SDG 13)** across countries and over time?”*

This project moves beyond surface-level data visualization. It acts as an **analytical bridge** between raw global data and causal inference, using inferential statistics to challenge misconceptions about what truly drives national carbon output.

---

## 🛠️ The Analytical Engine
We leveraged **Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Regression** to decompose the drivers of CO2 emissions.

### 📊 Scientific Foundations
| Explanatory Variable | Literature Grounding | Expected Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Renewable Energy %** | Apergis & Payne (2010) | 📉 Strong Reductive |
| **Urbanization Rate** | Poumanyvong & Kaneko (2010) | 📉 Efficiency Gain |
| **GDP Per Capita** | Environmental Kuznets Curve | 📈 Economic Scaling |

## 🌟 Dashboard Highlights
* **Dynamic Time-Filtering:** Explore 20+ years of climate data with instant reactive UI updates.
* **Myth vs. Reality:** A dedicated panel that confronts common public climate assumptions with hard regression coefficients.
* **Correlation Diagnostics:** A matrix to identify hidden multicollinearity between macro-economic indicators.
* **Longitudinal Trends:** Dual-axis plotting to visualize the causal interaction between energy transition and carbon release.
